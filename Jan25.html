Natsu (Summer)

by Takayanagi Katsuya

Another day, another search for a doujin that can fill the “If We Could Meet by the Sea” shaped hole in my heart.

That’s not fair, this story shares a lot more in common with the comic “Natsu to Jun” which I reviewed back on the 10th of January. The nostalgia of a childhood friendship blossoming into romance over the summer is a well worn topic of hentai, but I think this one does it well, however I do think it doesn’t hit the highs that the previous Natsu title did.

The art is one thing that is great in a vacuum but lacking when compared with the best of the genre. The lack of background fixtures and textures isn’t too bad, as a lot of doujins don’t take that step for emersion, but when the reaction faces are underplayed and chill, it feels more like something is missing than a minimalist choice.

The story itself is fine, but I can’t shake the feeling that there’s something off about their interaction. It’s hot, don’t get me wrong, but the escalation of events really throws me for a look sometimes. IDK, maybe if I was into drinking water from between girls legs, it’d be sweet, but as it stands, eh, it just felt funky.

I’m probably coming off a lot more negative than I should be about this work, but something about it didn’t click with me. Sorry, but I couldn’t get behind this one.

2 out of 4, damn I wanted to like this one a lot more than I did.
